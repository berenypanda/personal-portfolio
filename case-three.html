Description

Details

A month-long team project for my Database Management Systems class.

Objective

  • To design a database system for the fictional Universal Furniture Outlet, which sells home and office furniture.
  • Create a functioning Oracle mySQL database based on the team’s data models.

Journey

Developing the Entity-Relationship Diagram

  • Identifying Entities
    • The first step in designing a database system is knowing what objects are within it. To find entities, my team searched for nouns within the business description. Anything that described a person, place, or thing (excluding traits of potential objects, such as phone number) was classified as a potential entity.
  • Attributes
    • To determine the attributes, we included only the information about each object the business needs to function. These were also given in the description.

Relational Schema

  • Primary Keys
    • Every PK should uniquely identify each instance of an entity.
    • Some PK’s were explicitly given (Customer account numbers on the form are unique). For other PK’s, we focused on anything that we knew no two instances of an entity would share. In most business cases, these are usually ID numbers given to an employee or a product.
  • Foreign Keys
    • After determining which entities were connected, we linked the foreign keys of tables to the primary keys they were connected with.

SQL Metadata

  • With the relational schema developed, we now essentially had our tables. The additional work was determining the data type and the null constraints.

SQL Queries

  • My main contribution was generating queries. Select queries were relatively simple. Creating ? tables were initially challenging.

Conclusion

What I'd Do Differently

  • While I got to analyze the final product afterwards, I think being involved in every step of the process as a team would be ideal. As I take higher level information systems classes, I’ve come to realize the importance of assessing systems in a team. Other people can point out discrepancies or errors early, prevent mishaps down the road.
